Copy-Making
Essay, booklet (12 pages, saddle stitch with loop staples; first edition of 20, second edition of 15), posters
Booklet 5.5 x 8.5"
Posters 24 x 36"
2014

   

   

Copy-Making: The Copy in Contemporary Graphic Design is a proposed exhibition I curated in response to Ellen Lupton and Andrew Blauvelt's Graphic Design: Now in Production. Copy-Making showcases contemporary designers who utilize the inherent generative nature of graphic design to create work that challenges fine art's sacrosanct original and celebrates design as a standalone discipline. The traditional notion of the copy is transformed; the throwaway byproduct becomes an exploratory multi-dimensional space.

The booklet is a guide to the iteration of the show installed at the RISD Museum and uses my essay as a critical lens. The first edition was produced live at the museum as part of Design the Night.

The images on the posters are actual-size reproductions of a catalog for the show that I produced (not pictured). The posters themselves functioned as advertisements for my proposed exhibition within a real show of all the proposed exhibitions conceptualized by the class. They were hung across from each other to reiterate the idea of the copy in physical space.
